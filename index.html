Freshwater: Amazon River

The most diverse freshwater ecosystem in the world

The Amazon River is the second largest river in the world. Located in central South America, the Amazon River ecosystem is home to amazingly diverse life. The Amazon and its tributaries flow through the countries of Peru, Bolivia, Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, and Brazil.

The Amazon River Basin is home to some of the most diverse life in the world. It houses about 2,200 species of fish, 1,300 species of birds, 400 species of reptiles, 425 species of mammals, 430 species of amphibians, and 40,000 species of plants. The Amazon is home to plants with all different types of diets. Below is a list of some of these amazing organisms.

Human Interaction

The Amazon River has been used by humans for hundreds of years, providing everything from medicine, water, and even electricity. However, human influence is not always positive. Pollution and deforestation negatively impact the ecosystem, driving out many indigenous peoples and species.

Positive

Negative

Humans have used plants native to the Amazon River, such as Clavillia and Icoja Bark, as medicine for generations.

People have settled the Amazon for hundreds of years. Major population centers such as Iquitos and Manaus rely on the river for the succes of their communities.

The Amazon is a huge energy source for those in South America. The Itaipú Dam provides 12,600 megawatts of power, more than enough to power all of New York City.

Almost all deforestation in the Amazon River ecosystem is due to human influence, whether related to climate change or the direct clearing of land.

The land belonging to native Amazonians is shrinking. Less than 100 Tagaeri natives still continue to live their nomadic styled lives.

Huge wildfires are no longer an oddity in the Amazon. As people continue to deforest the Amazon using fire, accidental wildfire have become common.

Glossary

Basin - The area of land that is drained by a river and its tributaries

Dam - A barrier built for holding back water or diverting the flow of water.

Depth - How deep the water is.

Flood - Flooding happens when a river has too much water in its channel. The river will end up overflowing.

Freshwater - Water that has no salt in it.

Pollution - Materials that are not supposed to be in the river that make it dirty.

River - A river is a naturally winding watercourse that drains surplus water from a drainage basin.
