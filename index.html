Freshwater: Amazon River

The most diverse freshwater ecosystem in the world

The Amazon River is the second largest river in the world. Located in central South America, the Amazon River ecosystem is home to amazingly diverse life. The Amazon and its tributaries flow through the countries of Peru, Bolivia, Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, and Brazil.

The Amazon River Basin is home to some of the most diverse life in the world. It houses about 2,200 species of fish, 1,300 species of birds, 400 species of reptiles, 425 species of mammals, 430 species of amphibians, and 40,000 species of plants. The Amazon is home to plants with all different types of diets. Below is a list of some of these amazing organisms.

Manatee, Discus Fish, Angelfish, Zebra Pleco, Electric Eel, Dwarf Caiman, Anaconda, Goliath Tiger Fish, Red-Bellied Piranha, Red-Tail Catfish, Arowana, Arapaima Gigas

Adaptations

The Amazon is an extremely unique ecosystem, and as such, the life that exists there has greatly adapted to better fit in with their environment.

Animals

The River Otter has a dense layer of fat under it's skin and oily skin that protects it in extremely cold weather.

The Amazon River Dolphin has stiff hairs on its beak. These hairs are a sensory organ that help the Inia sense prey in muddy rivers.

Yellow-Spotted Amazon River Turtles have a hard neck which they bare at predators to protect themselves rather than pulling their heads into their shells.

Plants

Lianas start their lives on the top of canopy and have their roots drape down to the ground to get mroe sunlight.

The Amazon Water Lilly has sharp prickles on the bottom of the plant, encouraging animals not to eat the plant.

The Water Hyacinth has ribbed leaves that help it quickly cover the water surface and spread along the river base.
